# About Jonathan Shock

**Position & Affiliation**

Jonathan Shock holds the rank of Associate Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Applied Mathematics at the University of Cape Town.

**Research Interests**

His scholarly work moves between theoretical physics, complex systems, and the application of machine learning to problems across the sciences and humanities.

**Leadership Roles**

He directs [Shocklab](https://shocklab.net), a research group of students and postdocs working across a wide range of topics. He also serves as Interim Director of the UCT AI Initiative, Co-PI of the African Hub on AI Safety, Peace and Security, and PI of the African Compute Initiative.

**Contact & Web Presence**

For inquiries, he can be reached at [jon.shock@gmail.com](mailto:jon.shock@gmail.com). More information is available at [shocklab.net](https://shocklab.net).

**About this course**

These notes form the basis of MAM2046W, the second-year course on nonlinear dynamics at UCT. The course is loosely based on Steven Strogatz's *Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos*. Shock has taught this course over multiple years and the notes have evolved through iteration with students. The original notebooks were written in Wolfram Mathematica and have been converted into this web format with the help of Claude (Anthropic's AI assistant) — animations are exported as GIFs, mathematical typesetting uses KaTeX, and all Mathematica source code is hidden except where the code itself is the pedagogical content.

If you find any mistakes, or have comments on the notes, please send a message.
